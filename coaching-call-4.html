Step 1 · Niche (1 of 2)

Pick your niche.

Niching down lets you specialise, charge premium, and become “the guy.” First, judge the money side:

  • →
    Ticket size first. One client should be able to pay for the whole project, and a second makes you both profitable. High-ticket beats high-volume.
  • →
    Industry size, but don’t obsess. Big enough to have room, not so big it’s saturated. A smaller “blue ocean” can still print.
  • →
    Recession proof? Will they still spend when things tighten? (The trampoline park shut down in COVID.)
  • →
    Already done work there? Existing momentum is a strong signal.
Step 1 · Niche (2 of 2)

Pick your niche.

  • →
    B2C over B2B. Consumer-facing businesses actually use the website to win trust and customers, so a great site is worth far more to them.
  • →
    Standardised blueprint. Are the businesses similar enough to systemise? (Window cleaning = yes. “Insurance” = too many different sub-types.)
  • →
    Who benefits most from a website? High-commitment, “serious” decisions where the customer researches before buying. Surgeons, hair transplants, roofing, dentists, med spas.

The more a customer researches before they buy, the more a trust-building site is worth.

Then Commit

Become the guy.

Once you’ve made an informed decision, go with it. Plant the flag publicly. Don’t wait to feel ready.

“I’m the [niche] website guy.”

Post it. Specialising is what opens the doors. Networks, referrals, premium pricing. It’s a stepping stone, not a life sentence. Don’t be afraid to claim it.

Roofing & King Contractor = the proof-of-concept, not the only niche.

Step 2 · Platform

Where does your niche hang out?

You can’t fish where there are no fish. Find the watering hole before you write a single message, and log it all in a sheet.

Search “[niche] owners” on Facebook→ Find the groups & check they’re popping→ Note FB / IG / LinkedIn / forums

Different demographics live in different places. B2C trades cluster on Facebook; clinics & pro services skew LinkedIn/Instagram. Go where they are, then build your sheet of prospects.

Step 3 · Research

Study the winners.

Look at the agencies already winning in your niche. They’re “just” web designers, but what they actually sell is a strategy that delivers value. Your job is to uncover that blueprint and replicate it.

On The Competitor
How they operate

What they price, how they position, where they’re niche vs. generic.

On Their Website
What earns trust

Trust badges, reviews, real-life video, case studies (situation → solution → result), CTAs & friction.

Watch for the big tell: are they selling features, or results? Sell results.

Step 3 · Deep Research

They don’t want a website.

Research what your niche really wants from a marketer or web designer, and the exact words they use. Mirror that verbiage back in your offer and on calls.

The Surface Ask
“A new website”
Nice design, modern look, mobile-friendly.
→
The Real Goal
Trust & customers
Be chosen over competitors. More booked jobs. Look like the obvious safe choice.

Collect their phrases now. They become your headlines later.

The Lens

Traffic · Trust · Conversion.

Every successful website does three things. This is the framework you use to review sites, build your strategy, and explain your value to a prospect.

01
Traffic
Get the right people to the site. SEO, ads, presence.
02
Trust
Make them believe. Reviews, proof, faces, badges, results.
03
Conversion
Make it easy to act. Clear CTAs, frictionless contact.

Layer your unique mechanism into each. This same framework = great content.

Step 4 · The Offer

Build an irresistible offer.

The offer changes with your stage of business. Early on, you carry the risk to win trust; as your proof grows, you shift the risk back to them.

Stage 1  Free (portfolio + experience)→ Stage 2  Free upfront (pay if you like it)→ Stage 3  Deposit down→ Stage 4  Payment plan→ Stage 5  Raise prices

Reference: Alex Hormozi, “$100M Offers.”

The Beginner Power Move

The free-upfront offer.

You build a live homepage and have the proposal ready before you ever get on the call. They only pay if they love it. It feels insane to the prospect, which is exactly why it cuts through the spam.

Why It Works
  • Removes all risk from the prospect.
  • Proves your confidence and seriousness.
  • It’s tangible. A real, live homepage, not a mockup.
Why It’s Safe For You
  • ~$5 to generate a fully functional live homepage before the call (Website Factory).
  • No developer until it’s sold.
  • Small risk, huge upside.

Goal of the offer = sell the call, not the website.

Anatomy

How the offer is built.

  • Compliment
    Commend their reviews / work. Specific & genuine.
  • Pattern break
    “This’ll sound crazy, but hear me out…”
  • The offer
    A free, custom live homepage. No payment, no risk.
  • The “catch”
    You get a portfolio piece; you’re that confident.
  • The process
    You see the live homepage + proposal up front; if you like it, we build out the full site at an agreed price.
  • Risk reversal
    “If I waste your time, coffee’s on me.” Mutual win.

Keep it short, hyper-personal, and human. Never a wall of text.

Step 5 · Authority

Make your profile authoritative.

Before you reach out, people will check you. Give them a reason to trust you in 5 seconds. People do business with people who look reputable.

  • →
    Content. Post case studies, results, and website reviews using Traffic / Trust / Conversion. Become “the [niche] website person” publicly.
  • →
    Images. Your face, your work, reviews, examples. Visual proof beats claims.
  • →
    Position as an equal. Confident, helpful, excited for their business. Never an energy leech.
Step 5 · Booking

Make it easy to book you.

Remove every gram of friction between “interested” and “on a call.” When it turns positive, you book them in the same conversation.

Pick Any Tool
Calendar + video

Calendly, Google Meet, Zoom, or any calendar that lets you share a link and hop on a video call. Use what’s easy.

Wire It Up
Feed it into GHL

Capture name, email & number → GoHighLevel handles the reminders & follow-ups automatically.

Always schedule the next step while you still have them in the conversation.

Deep Dive · Abou’s Gold

The cold call.

Lowest cost, fastest feedback, most reps with real prospects. The goal of the call is not to sell a website. It’s to sell the next step.

DM / Call→ sell the call→ Call→ sell the Zoom→ Zoom→ sell the website

Move them along the natural progression of trust. Don’t rush steps. Every prospect you talk to teaches you the niche, even the ones who don’t buy.

The Acquisition Loop

The cold-call machine.

A repeatable manual loop you run daily. Manual first, systemise later.

Google Maps (Apify)→ Call→ Script→ Book call→ Generate live homepage

Pull businesses from Maps, dial them, run your script, book the call, and generate a live homepage (~$5) to present. Repeat at volume.

Feed The Machine · Apify

Build the lead list.

Apify is already in your Website Factory stack. Its Google Maps actor turns the manual “find a business” step into an instant pipeline. Set up a project in Claude and let it do the digging.

Target
Maps + niche + area
Pull businesses in your niche near you that have no website or a low-quality one. Your warmest cold prospects.
Scrape
Contact + socials
Grab email, phone number, and any social profiles you can reach out on.
Assemble
Claude builds the list
Claude cleans it into a ready-to-dial lead sheet for your script.

Cost: under ~$5 for 100 leads. Cheap fuel for the cold-call machine.

The Operating Law

Manual first.
Systemise later.

Do it by hand until you know it works, then automate.

Volume + reps beat clever tooling early on. Talk to prospects, learn the niche, build the proof. The systems come after the manual version is proven.
