The playbook

4 ways to get customers from groups

Method 01

DM outreach

Hand-pick qualified members and message them one by one. Slow. Best for reps and learning the niche.

Method 02

Group posts

Post the same offer across many groups. The beginner workhorse. First 5 to 10 customers live here.

Method 03

Partner with owners

Use someone else's audience. Pay-per-close or recurring split. Faster than building your own.

Method 04

Start your own group

Own the audience and the rules. Slowest to build. Largest moat once built.

Different effort curves. Same offer underneath.
Before any of the four

Step zero: a mini personal brand

Every method below assumes people will check you. They will click your name, look at your profile, and decide in five seconds whether to reply. Give them a reason to. This is the same "look reputable" rule from the Foundation, applied to Facebook.

No profile, no replies. No replies, no calls.
Five minute setup

What your profile needs

  • 01The right profile. Spin up a new one if your existing profile is full of family and high-school friends. Keep work clean.
  • 02A banner that signals what you do, the niche you serve, and your offer in one glance.
  • 03A bio that says it plainly: who you help, what you ship, how to book you.
  • 04A professional headshot. Real face, clear photo, good lighting. No avatars, no logos.
  • 05A few proof posts. Testimonials, results, website mock-ups, niche-specific tips. You do not need a daily cadence. You need to look real and competent.
Method 01 · DM outreach

Hand-pick and DM

Join the groups in your niche, find the businesses that fit your offer, review their site, then send a thoughtful message. Quality over blast. One person at a time.

Join the group→ Qualify the business→ Review their site→ Send the DM→ Book the call
The honest take

Slow, but it teaches you

DM outreach is the hardest method to scale. You will book a handful of calls and likely close one or two before you get tired of typing. Use it for reps and to learn what your niche actually responds to, then move to volume methods.

Treat DMs as research as much as acquisition.
Method 02 · Group posts

The beginner workhorse

This is what took the channel from zero to roughly $2.5K per month. You post the same offer (or close variations of it) across many relevant groups, then DM the people who comment. Same day, every time.

Your first 5 to 10 customers come from here, faster than from anywhere else.
The template

The post itself

Big bold colored background. Short hook. Specific number. Clear deliverable. Risk reversal baked in. The whole post fits in a single thumb scroll, which is the only thing standing between a yes and a swipe.

"Looking for 3 contractors in need of a free website build. We are expanding our portfolio. I promise you will love the site."
Plug in your niche. Keep it short. Make the offer hard to ignore.
How the offer monetises

Free build → monthly subscription

When you close someone from a post, you put them on a 14 to 30 day free trial. After the trial they continue on a recurring plan, typically $80 to $100 per month for a hosted site or GHL stack. Same offer logic as the live-homepage close: remove all risk to make the yes easy.

Group post→ Comment→ DM→ Trial→ Recurring
What goes wrong

You're at Facebook's mercy

  • →Admin gates. Many posts get blocked before they go live. Most niche groups have post-approval queues run by the owner.
  • →Throttling. When too many of your posts get removed, Facebook quietly lowers your reach across the whole platform.
  • →Silent DM failures. When a post pops off, your messages to non-friends sometimes silently fail to deliver. Whole sales cycles die quietly.
  • →Account warm-up matters. Do not burn a fresh profile by spamming 20 groups on day one. Ease in.
Hit or miss is the nature of the channel. Build for it.
When you are starting out

Still the best beginner method

If you cannot make hundreds of cold calls a day, group posts are a far better use of your time. Cheap fuel, real prospects, faster niche feedback than dialing strangers from a Maps scrape.

Volume + reps. Same law as cold call. Different surface.
Method 03 · Partner up

Partner with group owners

Most owners of niche groups are not monetising them. They built an audience and never figured out the offer. You are the offer. They bring the reach, you bring the close.

Find groups in your niche. Reach out to the owner with a clean proposal.
Two ways to split it

How the deal is built

Option A

Pay-per-close

Owner books or surfaces leads. You pay them a flat fee per closed deal. Clean, simple, results-aligned. Easy to track.

Option B

Subscription split

Owner gets a monthly cut of every recurring customer they bring you. E.g. $50/mo of a $200/mo plan. Long-term alignment, messier accounting.

An 80,000-member group can book all your calls. You just need to close.
The pitch

They built the audience.
You bring the offer.

Group owners want their group to feel valuable to members. A clean, results-driven offer with risk reversal makes them look good. Frame it as "I'll help your members win," not "let me sell to your list."

Lead with the member benefit. Money follows.
Method 04 · Own the audience

Start your own group

The long game. A niche-specific group where you own the rules. You decide who can post and who gets blocked. Drip Jobs (a painter SaaS) runs a 250,000-member group of painters they sell software to. Inbound leads, forever.

Slowest to build. Largest moat once built.
The compound effect

One group = infinite at-bats

  • →Sell on your terms. Promote your offer to your own audience as often as makes sense.
  • →Block competitor posts. Force approval on anything that smells like outreach. Keep the group clean.
  • →Free warm leads. Every new member is a pre-qualified, in-niche prospect at zero CAC.
  • →The group becomes the moat. Over time, it stops being a channel and starts being the product distribution itself.
This is what acquisition infrastructure looks like.
Why Facebook groups

The pros

Pro 01

Free acquisition

No ad spend. Pure organic. You pay in time and reps, not media.

Pro 02

No dialing required

Skip cold call volume entirely if you do not have hours a day to be on the phone.

Pro 03

Inbound at scale

Partner deals and your own group produce inbound leads without paid traffic.

Pro 04

Beginner-friendly

Fastest path to the first 5 to 10 customers without an existing track record.

Eyes open

The cons

Con 01

Price-sensitive

Group members tend to shop on price. Plan your offer (and your stack) so you can win on value at a fair price.

Con 02

Platform risk

You are renting the audience. Facebook can throttle posts or DMs at any time, with no warning.

Con 03

Solo ceiling

Methods 1 and 2 cap quickly. There is a real limit to how many posts and DMs one person can run.

Con 04

Need to embed

Real results come from being a regular contributor, not a tourist parachuting in with offers.

How to break the ceiling

Group infiltration = scale

Methods 1 and 2 plateau quickly. Real scale comes from partnerships or owning the group. Pick a partner play first because it is faster, then start growing your own group in parallel for the moat.

Why it matters
Run group posts hard for 30 days. Then layer the others on.

Spreading thin across all four ways kills momentum. Pick group posts first. Stack DM outreach as research. Bring in partners once you can demonstrate the offer closes. Open your own group when you have proof to seed it with.
